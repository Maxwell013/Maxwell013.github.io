FIRST, an introduction

I started my programming journey as a member of my school's FIRST Lego League (FLL) team. Delving into the basics of coding and robotics within this dynamic and engaging environment ignited my curiosity for technology and problem solving. From building and programming small robots to complete various tasks, I quickly developed a passion for the creative possibilities of programming.


This early experience paved the way for my transition to the FIRST Robotics Competition (FRC) team. Here, I found myself immersed in a world of real-world programming challenges and engineering marvels. Collaborating with teammates, I tackled complex problems, from optimizing robot movements to integrating sensors and systems for precise control.


Being part of the FRC team taught me valuable lessons in precision, efficiency, and teamwork. Working under pressure during competitions, I honed my skills in troubleshooting, debugging, and optimizing code for peak performance. Every challenge became an opportunity for growth, pushing me to think critically and innovate solutions in a fast-paced environment.


Today, as a programmer, I draw upon the foundation laid by my experiences with the FLL and FRC teams. They not only sparked my passion for programming but also instilled in me the resilience and adaptability needed to thrive in the ever-evolving field of software development.
